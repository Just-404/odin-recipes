Mangu

Description:

Mangu is the most popular plantain-based dish in the Dominican Republic. It's a traditional Dominican breakfast/dinner recipe, which consists of a creamy, smooth plantain mash, accompanied with eggs and a type of sausage called salami. If you try it for the first time, you won't regret it.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ripe plantains
  • 1Â½ teaspoons salt
  • 4 tablespoons butter or olive oil.

Steps

  1. Peel the plantains, cut lengthwise, and then divide each half into two.
  2. Place the plantains and water in a saucepan. Bring to a boil, and cook 20 minutes, until plantains are tender but slightly firm.
  3. Remove the plantains from the water and mash them right away with a fork until they are very smooth and there are few to no lumps.
  4. Finally, mix in butter, and progressively add a cup of water at room temperature and keep mashing and mixing until it turns into a very smooth puree.
  5. (Optional) For full experience serve with eggs and salami.
